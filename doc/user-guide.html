bgdude user guide

A page-by-page walk-through of every screen, panel and icon — what it's for and how to use it. bgdude reads your t:slim X2 and CGM, pulls health data, predicts glucose and advises on dosing. It is read-only: it never delivers insulin — you always dose on the pump yourself.

Getting started

First launch runs a short onboarding, then you land on the Today tab.

Onboarding (4 steps)

  • Intro — what the app does.
  • Before you pair — you must tick the acknowledgement: pairing with the pump unpairs the official t:connect app, the connection is reverse-engineered (can be finicky), and the app never sends insulin/control commands.
  • About you — pick your glucose units (mmol/L or mg/dL) up front so every reading shows the way you think about your numbers, plus an optional profile (sex, age, diabetes type/duration, weight, height). You can skip the profile and set it later in Profile, and change units any time in Settings.
  • Get connected — the final gate. You can't finish until you either pair your pump or choose demo mode. "Pair your pump" scans over Bluetooth and prompts for the pairing code; "Use demo mode" runs the app on simulated data. Pairing also grants Health Connect + pump/notification permissions.
No hardware? Choose demo mode on the last onboarding step to run against a simulated t:slim X2 + Dexcom. It seeds roughly two weeks of history, so every screen is populated — the live Today view plus all the range-based reports and insights (AGP, GMI/A1c, sleep, correlations, model accuracy, meals). Demo mode can only be entered here — there's no manual switch in Settings. The DEMO badge and an Exit demo button appear in the top bar; tap it (or Settings → Demo mode → Exit) to switch back to your real pump when you're ready.

Home-screen widget

Glucose at a glance, no app open

Add the bgdude widget to your Android home screen to see your latest glucose without opening the app. Long-press the home screen → Widgets → bgdude, and drop it in (it's a 2×1 cell and resizes).

What it shows

  • Glucose + trend arrow in your chosen unit (mmol/L or mg/dL — it follows the in-app setting), colour-coded by range: green in range, red below 70 mg/dL (3.9), orange above 180 mg/dL (10.0).
  • IOB — insulin on board — and how long ago the reading was taken ("7m ago").
  • If the reading is missing or older than 15 minutes it greys out and reads "Stale · Xm ago" (or "no data"), so a frozen number never looks current.
  • Tap the widget to open the app.
The widget updates whenever a new CGM reading arrives while the app/service is running, and refreshes the "ago" text on its own — the times and grey-out stay honest even between readings and after a launcher restart.

Garmin watch

bgdude pushes your glucose to a Garmin watch over the free Connect IQ Mobile SDK (the same phone→watch path xDrip+ uses — no paid Garmin Health API).

Privacy. Unlike the rest of bgdude (which stays on your phone), the watch path routes your BG and IOB through the Garmin Connect app. Garmin Connect may sync that data to Garmin's cloud under Garmin's own privacy policy — so enabling the Garmin features means your glucose/IOB can leave the device via Garmin. Leave the Garmin integration off if you want everything to remain strictly on-device.
Widget & glance
Watch face
Data field (activity screen)

Rendered in the Connect IQ simulator (fēnix 8) with a sample reading.

Three ways to see it on the watch

  • Widget (+ glance) — a full panel: unit, range-coloured BG with trend arrow, delta + reading age, and IOB / pump battery / reservoir. The glance is a one-liner (7.3 → +0.6 IOB 1.4).
  • Watch face — time + date with a range-coloured BG line (value, trend, delta, IOB/age); drops per-second work in low-power mode.
  • Data field — range-coloured BG on an activity screen, font auto-scaled to the field size.
  • All three share one source of truth for units, range colours (red < 70 mg/dL, green 70–180, amber > 180) and the trend arrow, and follow your in-app unit (mmol/L or mg/dL). Data older than 15 minutes greys out and is marked "(stale)".
  • The widget receives phone pushes in the foreground; the watch face and data field can't, so they use a small background service that stores each reading for the view to pick up. Readings are queued by Connect IQ, so the last value shows the moment the view refreshes even if it arrived while closed.
The watch companion isn't on the Connect IQ store — it's the project under garmin/, built and side-loaded with Garmin's Connect IQ SDK (see garmin/README.md). Like everything else, it's display-only — the watch never doses.

Today

The Today tab

Your Day panel

A plain-language narrative of how the day is going — current stats, trends and a suggestion or two. Read it first thing.

Glucose & "Next few hours" chart

  • The big number is your current glucose with its trend arrow.
  • The Next few hours chart shows a trailing CGM trace leading into the forward forecast. The y-axis is glucose in your chosen unit (mmol/L or mg/dL); the dashed vertical line is now; the green band is your target range; the x-axis marks -2h, now, +2h. A small legend names each line.
  • Below it, an On board chart projects the insulin (IOB) and carbs (COB) already working — plus your scheduled basal — over the next few hours. These are the drivers behind the glucose forecast. Insulin reads on the left axis (U, U/h), carbs on the right (g).
  • Tip: touch-and-hold anywhere on a line to read the exact time and value (with units) in the tooltip; on the scenario chart the tooltip names each line.

Rescue-carb card

Appears only when you're low or a low is predicted. It suggests fast carbs (≈15 g, adjusted for insulin-on-board and the predicted nadir). Informational — act on the pump/with food yourself.

The event stream (timeline)

Scroll down for the day's events — meals, boluses, highs/lows, detected unannounced rises, and compression lows. Each card can do two things:

  • Explain this — opens ranked, plain-language reasons for a high/low/rise you can accept with one tap.
  • Tag (use for model) — tap to tell the model how to treat the event. A smart default is already applied: pump dosing (meals, boluses) is used by default; a suspected compression low is excluded by default. The reasons offered are contextual to the event type — a compression low is never offered "missed carbs".
Tagging an event "ignore" writes a note that keeps the model from learning the wrong lesson (and, for confirmed carbs, adds them to your history).

Predict

Forecast horizons & scenarios
  • Horizon forecasts (30 / 60 / 120 min) with uncertainty bands — a physiological baseline plus a learned correction once trained.
  • Scenario lines (in Advanced mode) — the same glucose forecast under four different assumptions about what's driving you: IOB (insulin only), COB (insulin + logged carbs), UAM (an unannounced meal is pushing you up), and Zero-temp (basal suspended). They spread apart because the assumptions differ — the gap is the uncertainty. The bold Predicted line is the best single estimate. A colour legend labels each, and tapping the chart shows every line's value (named, with units) at that moment. An On board chart of projected IOB/COB/basal sits below.
  • Sensitivity & risk read-outs — today's resistance multiplier and near-term low/high risk with a suggested action.
  • What-if explorer — sliders for carbs and insulin to project the trajectory before you decide.
  • Control-IQ aware. When the pump reports the closed loop is active, the forecast folds in its automatic basal changes and corrections — pulling projected highs and lows back toward the mode's target band (tighter in Sleep, higher in Exercise) instead of assuming an open loop. Rescue-carb advice uses the same softened forecast.

Insights

Insights, read together

Daily briefing

The morning summary: overnight time-in-range plus prescriptive, evidence-grounded notes ("consider bolusing a touch stronger").

Insulin sensitivity

Today's resistance multiplier and its drivers (short sleep, low HRV, illness, exercise, medication…). >1 = more resistant than your settings; <1 = more sensitive.

A1c goal & Lab A1c

  • Your GMI (CGM-derived A1c estimate) vs your goal, with a projection. Tap Change to set the goal.
  • Log lab A1c — enter a lab HbA1c result; the app compares it to your GMI and flags a glycation gap (some people's lab result runs systematically higher/lower than their average glucose predicts).

Sleep & glucose

How your sleep length relates to overnight steadiness, learned from your own nights.

Illness mode no longer lives here — it's auto-suggested in Confirm events and toggled from Quick-log. See Illness mode.

Meals

The saved-meal library

The library

Meals you eat often, each learning its own absorption curve and typical outcome over time. Search with the bar at top; tap a meal for its detail screen.

Add a meal

Tap Add meal. In the sheet you can:

  • Scan barcode — point the camera at a product; carbs (and fat/protein) fill in from the food database.
  • Search foods — type a name to look it up (packaged products + a bundled offline Australian generic-food set).
  • Scan nutrition label — photograph the nutrition panel (the facts table, not the food). The label text is read on-device (OCR; the photo never leaves your phone), the carbs/fat/protein/serving are parsed out, and — for unusual or foreign layouts — an optional small on-device AI model (Gemma, downloaded from Settings → Nutrition-label AI) can normalise the reading. It's a reading aid: values land in the editable fields for you to confirm, and it never doses anything by itself.
  • Every looked-up value lands in an editable field — correct anything the database got wrong, or adjust to your portion. Fat/Protein (optional) feed the fat-protein-unit coach.
Food lookup uses Open Food Facts (free, public). A one-time notice explains that only the barcode/text is sent — no health data. Turn it off in Settings → Barcode food lookup to stay fully offline. The nutrition-label scan is always fully on-device.

Meal detail & pre-bolus coach

Learned curve, insights & coach
  • Log this meal — records that you ate it; the app measures the outcome ~3 h later and refines the curve.
  • Pre-bolus coach — from the live reading, how many minutes to pre-bolus.
  • Fat/protein card — for fat/protein-heavy meals, a split/extended-dose suggestion and a delayed-rise watch (3–5 h).
  • Insights & recent outcomes — median peak, time-above-range, and whether pre-bolusing has helped this meal.

Bolus advisor

Always shows its working

Open it from the Bolus button on Today. Enter carbs (and it reads your current glucose + IOB); it suggests a dose and always shows the working — BG, target, ISF, carb ratio, IOB and the context multiplier.

  • Fat & protein. Optionally add the meal's fat/protein — either exact grams, or a quick load picker (None / Low / Med / High) when you don't know the grams. The advisor works out the fat-protein units and suggests an extended dose (e.g. "+2.5 U over 5 h") to cover the delayed "pizza effect" rise — shown separately from the dose-now figure, since it's best given as a combo/dual-wave bolus. It also flags the delayed rise and a possible later low.
  • It leans conservative near predicted lows and never suggests a negative dose (that's a rescue-carb situation).
  • Control-IQ aware. When the closed loop is running it also corrects highs and eases off for lows automatically. So in Standard/Exercise mode the advisor halves a standalone correction (to avoid stacking with the pump's auto-corrections) and says so; in Sleep mode — where Control-IQ won't auto-correct — it keeps the full correction and flags that a manual one may be needed.
  • It never delivers insulin — enter the dose on your pump yourself.

Quick-log

Fast context capture

The ⊕ button in the top bar. Log the things the models want to know:

  • Carbs / bolus you took (that the pump didn't capture).
  • Wellbeing — exercise, alcohol, stress, mood (great/ok/low), and illness. Logging alcohol also opens an overnight-low watch (alcohol causes delayed lows) and a heads-up.
  • 🤒 Illness — turn illness mode on/off here (pick Mild / Moderate / Severe). It raises expected insulin needs while sick and tags the period for the models; end it when you're better. The app also auto-suggests illness in Confirm events when your data looks illness-like.
  • Sensor / infusion-site change — resets the age tracker used for reminders and the site-failure story.

Confirm events

Turn detections into confirmed data

Settings → Confirm events (a badge shows how many are waiting). The app queues detected-but-unconfirmed events — unannounced meals, compression lows, illness — for a one-tap decision.

  • Confirm — counts it as real, confirmed data in reports and teaches the model correctly (and adds a meal's carbs to history).
  • Edit — adjust a detected meal's carbs before confirming.
  • Dismiss — it won't come back.

Exercise mode

Announce a workout

Settings → Exercise mode. Tell the app about an upcoming or current workout so it can be proactive.

  • Pick a type (aerobic / resistance / mixed), a duration, and when it starts.
  • It raises the low-alert threshold for the session plus a recovery tail (longer for aerobic), so lows are caught earlier during and after.
  • It suggests a pre-exercise snack from your live glucose / insulin-on-board, and arms the overnight-low watch.
  • Tap End exercise mode when you're done (it also expires on its own).

Medication / steroid mode

Settings → Medication / steroid mode. Steroids raise insulin needs, often a lot.

  • Turn it on while you're on a course; pick an intensity (mild / moderate / high → +15 / 25 / 40% resistance).
  • The whole app then expects more resistance in its sensitivity, dosing hints and insights. Turn it off when the course ends. Advisory only — it never changes your pump.

Illness mode

Sick days raise insulin needs, so illness mode boosts the models' expected resistance while it's on and tags the period so a sick day doesn't mislead training.

  • Auto-detected. When your data looks illness-like (fever, raised resting heart rate, low HRV, dropped steps, higher glucose), the app raises a suggestion in Confirm events (and a notification). Confirm turns illness mode on; Dismiss clears it.
  • Turn it on yourself from Quick-log → 🤒 Illness — pick Mild / Moderate / Severe (how much to boost). Tap it again to End illness when you're better.
  • While a sustained high coincides with illness (or a likely site failure, or being very high with little insulin on board), a Check ketones alert fires with sick-day guidance.
Illness mode used to live on the Insights tab — it's now auto-suggested in Confirm events and toggled from Quick-log.

Weather

Settings → Weather. Opt-in ambient-temperature context (free, no account).

  • Type your city and tap search — it's geocoded once and stored.
  • Hot weather speeds insulin absorption (more lows) and cold has a paradoxical risk, so at temperature extremes low alerts lead a little earlier.
  • Daily temperature is also correlated against your glucose in the Correlations report.
Only the city/coordinates leave the device (to Open-Meteo). Turn the switch off to stop all weather lookups.

Notifications

Quiet hours, thresholds & per-category tuning

Settings → Notifications. Every alert is a category you control. All alerts are additive to your CGM/pump alarms — never a replacement.

Alert thresholds

Set your own low and high alert lines with the −/+ steppers (shown in your unit). Safety modifiers (age, alcohol, exercise, hot/cold weather) can only make the low alert lead earlier, never later.

Quiet hours

A do-not-disturb window that holds back non-critical notifications overnight. Urgent/predicted lows and pump alarms always come through. Toggle it on and set the From/To times.

Per-category controls

Every alert below is its own category you can tune: on/off, intensity (silent → urgent), sound, vibration, and a repeat-until-clear interval (Once / every 5 / 15 / 30 / 60 min). Categories marked † always come through even during quiet hours.

Pump

Everything synced from the pump

Settings → Pump. A read-only view of live and derived pump data:

  • Live status — glucose + trend, insulin-on-board, basal rate, last bolus, and Control-IQ which shows the closed loop's state and current mode (e.g. "Active · Sleep").
  • Insulin today — total, bolus/basal split.
  • Reservoir — units left + a runway estimate.
  • Site & sensor — age since last change.
  • Device — battery (with a time-to-empty estimate or "charging"), firmware, API version.
  • Active alarms/alerts and a recent pump-events timeline.

Protocol Explorer developer

Settings → Developer → Protocol Explorer. The Explorer lives in the Developer menu (a tucked-away home for low-level, read-only diagnostics) rather than the main Settings list. The whole Developer menu — and the raw protocol logcat dump behind it — appears in debug builds only; release builds don't show it. It's a console for probing the t:slim X2 Bluetooth protocol on a live pump — used to discover and verify pump reads the app doesn't otherwise surface. It has two tabs:

  • Requests — a grouped catalog of every read the pump supports (core status, documented opportunities like Home-screen mirror, Pump features, Pump settings, Control-IQ sleep schedule, and unknown/experimental reads like Secret menu). A coloured dot marks each request's status: in app (already surfaced), opportunity (documented, not yet surfaced), or unknown (undocumented — fire it to see what comes back). Tap Send to fire one. Two reads take parameters (IDP-segment and history-log range), which have inline number fields. The ▶ Sweep all reads toolbar button fires every non-parametric read in sequence — one tap to capture the pump's whole read surface into the Log.
  • Log — every message sent (↗) and received (↙), newest first. Each entry shows the message name, opcode, characteristic and cargo size; expand it to see the raw cargo bytes (hex) and pumpx2's decoded fields. Copy a single entry, or use the toolbar to copy the whole log or clear it.
Read-only, enforced in the native layer. The Explorer can only send unsigned CURRENT_STATUS requests that don't modify insulin delivery — the pump-comm layer refuses anything else by construction (it never builds a control/insulin-affecting message), so nothing here can change pump behaviour. Capture only runs while the screen is open. Pair your pump on the Pump screen first; the strip at the top shows the live connection state.

Glucose meter

Import fingersticks over Bluetooth

Settings → Glucose meter. Import fingerstick readings from a Bluetooth meter that speaks the standard Bluetooth Glucose Service — e.g. the Accu-Chek Guide / Guide Me (the same profile xDrip+ uses, so it isn't brand-specific). Imported readings are stored as calibration-type readings, kept distinct from your CGM.

  • Pair — put the meter in Bluetooth pairing mode (usually after a reading, or by holding its button), tap Scan for meter, pick it from the list, and confirm the pairing on the meter.
  • Sync now — pulls every reading the meter has stored since the last sync. It remembers each reading's sequence number, so re-syncing never imports a reading twice.
  • Recent fingersticks — the latest imported readings in your unit, plus a running total.
  • Unpair — forget the meter and reset the import history.
Each reading's time comes from the meter's own clock — if it has drifted from your phone, imported times can be slightly off. Like everything in bgdude it's read-only: it never sends anything to the meter, and the sync runs on demand (it doesn't hold the Bluetooth connection open).

Therapy profile & basal suggestions

Your pump IDP

Therapy profile

Settings → Therapy profile. Your basal rate, ISF, carb ratio and target per time-of-day segment (imported from the pump, editable). These feed the what-if engine, bolus advisor and predictions — getting them right matters.

Basal suggestions

From repeated fasting trends

Settings → Basal suggestions. From repeated carb-free (fasting) time-of-day trends, per-segment basal changes with a confidence and a rationale. Advisory only — make any change on the pump yourself and discuss with your team. It never writes to the pump.

Advanced / model internals

The engine, exposed
  • Advanced mode (Settings switch) — surfaces prediction decomposition and model internals throughout.
  • Model internals — effective sensitivity, the learned time-of-day profile, forecaster state and a Clarke error grid.
  • Forecast accuracy — how predictions have scored against actual outcomes (RMSE, Clarke A+B and hypo sensitivity per horizon; hypo sensitivity shows "—" when the window simply contained no lows to detect).
  • Retrain forecaster now — rebuild the learned correction from your history. A retrain also runs automatically about once a day at startup, and a new model is only promoted if it passes the safety gate and beats both the physics baseline and the currently active model on the same held-out data — so a retrain can never make the live forecast worse. The card shows the candidate / baseline / active RMSE from the last run and why a candidate was kept or rejected.

Profile

Details that tune the models

Settings → Profile. All optional, encrypted, on-device.

  • Sex — gates the menstrual-cycle features (they only apply when there's a cycle).
  • Age & years with diabetes — older age and long-standing diabetes make low alerts lead a little earlier (impaired-awareness risk).
  • Weight / height — reference (BMI).

Settings & integrations

The Settings hub

Everything not on a tab lives in Settings. Beyond the screens above:

  • Demo mode — when active (chosen during onboarding), a status row here lets you Exit back to your real pump. There's no switch to turn it on; that only happens in onboarding.
  • Glucose units — mmol/L (Australian default) or mg/dL. You pick this during onboarding; change it here any time and the whole app follows.
  • Sync health data now — pull sleep, HRV, resting/continuous HR, steps and workouts from Health Connect.
  • Barcode food lookup — the online food-lookup switch (see Meals).
  • Nutrition-label AI — optionally download a small on-device model (Gemma, ~0.5 GB) that helps the label scanner read unusual or foreign-language panels. Paste a Gemma 3 1B .task model URL (Gemma is licence-gated, so it isn't bundled) with an access token if the host needs one; it downloads once and runs entirely on your phone. Everything works without it — the deterministic parser is the always-on path.
  • Re-pair / Unpair pump — start a fresh scan or drop the pairing.
  • Nightscout — optional upload of entries, treatments and device status; enter your site URL + API secret and Test connection.

Safety & scope

Read-only. The app cannot deliver insulin or change your pump — enforced at the connection layer. You always dose on the pump yourself.
  • Predictions and suggestions are informational and can be wrong; they never replace your CGM/pump alarms or clinical advice.
  • The bolus advisor shows its working and leans conservative near lows.
  • This is a personal tool, not a medical device. Pairing with the pump unpairs the official app.
